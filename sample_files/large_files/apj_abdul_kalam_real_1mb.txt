
Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam) was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. 
He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. Before his presidency, he was a renowned aerospace scientist and 
played a key role in India’s civilian space program and military missile development efforts. He worked with the Defence Research 
and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).

Kalam was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite 
in near-Earth orbit in 1980. He was also deeply involved in developing ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology, earning him 
the nickname "Missile Man of India". He was a central figure in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests conducted in 1998.

He believed strongly in education, youth empowerment, and national development. His vision for India was captured in initiatives 
like Vision 2020, which aimed to transform India into a developed nation. He frequently interacted with students and encouraged 
them to dream big and work hard.

Some of his notable books include "Wings of Fire", "Ignited Minds", and "India 2020". These works reflect his journey, philosophy, 
and vision for the country. Kalam was widely respected for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to public service.

He received several awards including the Bharat Ratna (1997), India’s highest civilian award. Other honors include the Padma Bhushan 
and Padma Vibhushan. He remained active in teaching and mentoring even after his presidency.

Dr. Kalam passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His life continues 
to inspire millions across India and the world.
